Ilsa Tariq 21-Mar-20 11:16 AM
@blackmyst3ry Do you have problems concentrating? Do you have trouble in accepting the importance of your degree?
First off, let me tell you that your success or final outcome isn’t only determined by your degree. There are millions of people who have made themselves into something without a degree because they cultivated their strengths in other things. However, I will say this— studying and attaining a strong degree is the prime gateway that can lead you to that success without much hard work which other people might have to put in. Studying is hard work too, but far less hard work than what other people without a degree have to exercise. A good education from a reputable university showers you with confidence and respect in the eyes of many people, especially employers. I’m not saying that it’s the only way, but it sure is an easy way and an opportunity that has landed in your nest. So why not make the most out of it?
Personally speaking, my goal and motto in life has always been to make the most out of an opportunity. There are thousands of people around you who have spent the same as you to attain this world-class degree, and let me tell you this— they have the same potential as you, too! That’s a beautiful thing. And it is something powerful. But at the end of the day, some will fail miserably and drop out; some will perform outstanding and graduate. As cliche as this might sound— you need to delve deep into yourself and discover that unique potential within you whilst believing in the fact that you can conquer anything in the world. I’m not just playing with words. What I just said has meaning. Have conviction. One of the first steps is having he capacity to love and forgive yourself as well as promising yourself not to make the same mistakes again. It’s time to be genuine with yourself. You can lie to your friends, parents and family, as well as the seven billion people around you— but never yourself. So if you feel as if you can make the most out of life but are not working towards it, you are violating yourself and it’s not fair on you.
If you think that you’ve lost time, you’re wrong. I know people who have studied a month, or in exceptional cases, two weeks before the exams and ended up with merits and distinctions. So start now. Get out of your comfort zone. After reading this, go out for a walk solo or meditate and plan a schedule. Make sure to keep an hour or so for your hobbies. Do you have any interests? Could be Art, writing, a particular sport? If you do then make sure to inculcate it in your routine. Fix your routine. Sleep a couple hours after sunset and wake up fifteen minutes before sunrise. As much as people nowadays like living the nightlife and being nocturnal, you be the stronger man/lady and believe this: “early to bed and early to rise, makes a man healthy, wealthy, and wise.” This is the epitome of strength.
Eat healthy. The better and purer you eat, the stronger your mind and body. If you’re a junk food or fast food addict, start slowly; but set a strict aim and do not deviate. Start exercising, too. Apart from that, if you have problems in concentrating like me, spend a few minutes mediating and take very deep breaths. And don’t give up. Do not throw away your degree. There are millions— in fact, billions of people around you who yearn to have the same chance as you. They are also people who were in your exact same position as you but didn’t realise the importance of that privilege and are now struggling and falling off the ladders of life because they didn’t try pushing themselves earlier.
I know; I know— it’s difficult. Life is hard; but fight like a warrior. You can get through this. And please know this— there is point of studying because it expands your mind you cannot comprehend. Sure, there are other modes of knowledge and learning, but do not reject conventional studying itself. I get it, it can be boring and monotonous but sometimes you ought to do some of these things for the sake of your future, at least— if not anyone else’s or your future family tree’s. This is life. It’s tedious; but also very beautiful. We’re in this together!
As the poem by Douglas Mallach states: “Good timber does not grow with ease: The stronger wind, the stronger trees; The further sky, the greater length; The more the storm, the more the strength. By sun and cold, by rain and snow, In trees and men good timbers grow." My favourite.
