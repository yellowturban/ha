@cass 🇸🇬 For income effect depends on how much the change in real income(purchasing power) due to the change in price of good which would make you richer/poorer and hence you may want more of the good if income falls. (Basically understand what is your income and substitution effect) Income effect could be larger than substitution effect for Giffen Goods could be due to the fact that there are no close substitutes for the consumer to choose eg: if price of your staples(rice/potato) increases, you’ll spend more of your income buying staples rather than buying other types of food Due to cross price elasticity of demand, it determines the relationship between 2 goods, then you do the hicksian decomposition to find the substitution and income effects of the 2 goods
