morgenthau 🇵🇰 05-Jul-20 07:43 PM
see it depends. If its something that you feel that you could come up with yourself or is common sense/knowledge, then sure. But if its something that is not possible for you to know unless you read it somewhere, then reference it regardless of how many words you change. for example, lets say you're in a political science paper and are about to define the term "political sociology", then no matter how many words you move around, the examiner knows you read it somewhere. So after defining it, just add the original authors name in parantheses. However, if you're saying something like "politics is very important for well functioning societies bla bla", then it is okay not to reference it (as long as it isn't copy pasted). Hope that made sense
