Xeune 04-Dec-20 05:22 PM
Assume u got 2 goods in the economy(Y,X). If you have a lot of Y and I give you one more Y, would you value that Y more compared to when you have no Y but a lot of X. You would value Y more when you do not have Y at all. Next, if you have a lot of Y and I give you the option to trade the Y for 1 X good. How much you would trade? Assuming that you now have 100Y and no X. Would trade 10Y for 1 X? maybe? Assuming you did, the next trade I give the option again 10Y for 1X. Maybe now, it is not worth it anymore given you value 1X for 9Y. Okay so you don't trade now. Now I let you trade again, this time it would be 9Y for 1X. You would take this trade. Can you see that once you have more and more of X you value X less and less? Same goes with Y This is the principle of diminishing marginal rate of substation. (edited)
