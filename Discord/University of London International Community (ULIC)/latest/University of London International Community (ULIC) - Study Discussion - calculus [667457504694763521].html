Mo Ita 29-Mar-20 04:31 PM
I wouldn't say they are the same thing, only that one of the conditions implies the other. If a curve is tangential to a line, it is by implication touching it. The converse however is not true. But that's not the point, is it? Even if the two conditions are not exact reiterations of one other, it is still true that the first subsumes the second thereby rendering it redundant. Why not just say tangential and leave it at that? I suspect that this is to emphasise that proving tangency involves proving two statements. To show that a contour f(x,y)=c, has a point (x_0,y_0) at which the contour is tangential to the line y=mx+c, one must show that: 1. The derivative of the contour at (x_0,y_0) is equal to the derivative of the line, m. - TANGENTIAL 2. The point (x_0,y_0) lies on the line y=mx+c. - TOUCHING Hope this helps ! 1.
