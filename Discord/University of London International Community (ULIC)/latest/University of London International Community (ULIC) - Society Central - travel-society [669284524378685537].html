Today in Argentina it is the Day of Respect for Cultural Diversity. It is a day for introspection, to look back and think about the history of the country. It started as a way to remember the moment when the European explorers first arrived to America in the 15th century but now we celebrate the huge variety of cultures that converge in the country. Unfortunately, its indigenous populations have mostly disappeared but continue to be a big influence for the descendants that do remain. Argentina, as many others, is now a country of immigrants. Throughout the 20th century, it received big waves of immigration from countries such as Italy, Spain, Russia, Germany, Poland, Ukraine, France, Turkey, Armenia, China and Korea, and in more recent years, from neighbouring countries like Paraguay and Bolivia. It is hard to find someone who doesn‚Äôt have one, if not many, of these places as part of their ancestry. With them, come a lot of different beliefs and traditions that coexist in our society. So, today we take the day to reflect upon this and what we can do to further embrace the differences that make our country what it is. Where do you come from?
