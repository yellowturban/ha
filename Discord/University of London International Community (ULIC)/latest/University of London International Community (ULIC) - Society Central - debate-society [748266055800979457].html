@Debate Society Member DSMUN Winter Mock resources Delegates, apologies for the delay in resources posting. I am noticing a lot of anxiety for first-timer delegates, not wanting to actively participate in this session, hence I would like to address a few things: - Please note that this is an interactive MOCK training session on Position Papers and Motions. Don't worry about lacking experience. The purpose of this training session is specifically to accommodate novice delegates; it is beginner-friendly. - Position Paper to be submitted in this chat before the commencement of the session. This is optional, but highly recommended. - Position Paper help, how to put forth motions, how to address and feedback will be available during the session. Your Position Paper will help guide you through the opening speeches to be conducted during the session. - Without preparation, you will not be able to participate in this training session. If delegates are noticed to be significantly under prepared during the session, we unfortunately will have to ask the delegate(s) to leave the session. - Spectatorship is only possible for students who are already member on DS's Debate division. - You will conduct a research on the policies and stance of your assigned country on the topic, and to summarize it in a Position Paper. - Resources: Points and Motion guide http://munkiconference.weebly.com/points-and-motions.html (I understand that this one may not make much sense to you until you are in session, your Chair will guide you through it) Position Paper guide https://www.wisemee.com/how-to-write-a-mun-position-paper/ I hope this helps you better prepare for the session!
