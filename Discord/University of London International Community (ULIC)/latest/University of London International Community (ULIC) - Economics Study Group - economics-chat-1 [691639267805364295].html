Does anyone know if QE is used to lower interest rates, or to inject money into the economy directly via the repayments to bondholders?
Cookie Monster 10-May-20 10:35 PM
The main aim of QE inject money into the system buy buying bonds and securities, what differentiates QE from regular open market operations is the amount and the type of securities traded. During QE central banks buys non traditional securities in large amount. Interest rates are lowered before implementing any policy like QE as lower rates allows for easier borrowing. QE is typically implemented when interest rates are approaching zero because, at this point, central banks have fewer tools to influence economic growth, Its like a last resort to boost aggregate demand. (edited)

Xeune 11-May-20 03:20 PM
From what i know, it is when lowering interest rate are not working enough. For example, in 2007 before the housing crisis, interest rate in the US was around 5 to 4%. As housing loan starts to defaulted more and more, the fed lowered interest rate more and more to 0% at 16/12/2008. As we know when interest fall, investment start to increase however during the housing crisis no one wanted to invest due to the increase risk at that point of time. That was when the fed decide to do quantitative easing, which was purchasing of risker asset therefore increasing money supply. Money supply increases then ur LM shift to the right further lowering ur interest rate and increasing output. Hopefully im not repeating what the others had said.
